Swarm intelligence

What is swarm intelligence?

Swarm intelligence is the collective behavior of decentralized, self-organized systems, natural or artificial. SI systems consist typically of a population of simple agents or boids interacting locally with one another and with their environment. The inspiration often comes from nature, especially biological systems. The agents follow very simple rules, and although there is no centralized control structure dictating how individual agents should behave, local, and to a certain degree random, interactions between such agents lead to the emergence of "intelligent" global behavior, unknown to the individual agents. Examples of swarm intelligence in natural systems include ant colonies, bee colonies, bird flocking.

About the project:

In this project, I modeled an ant colony where the colony's task is to transport food to their home. The ants start at random points in their world and choose either finding food or returning home as their objective. During their search, they can only do two things: transmit information to others in their vicinity about when they last visited either of these two objectives and change direction based on the information received from others. In the event that an ant reaches its desired destination, it starts searching for the other target.

Algorithm:

  1. take a step
  2. increase all the counters by one
  3. if you bump into one of the items:
    1. reset the respective counter
    2. if this is the destination you were willing to reach:
      1. turn around 180 degrees
      2. change the destination objective in your head
  4. shout out the value of one of your counters plus the maximum distance you can be heard at
  5. listen to what the others are shouting
  6. if you have heard a value less than in your counter:
    1. update the respective counter
