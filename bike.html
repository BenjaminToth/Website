Bike courier project

About the project:

This project was based on addressing several issues faced by a delivery company. In the system of such a company, it collects restaurants that offer home delivery and provides customers with a convenient interface through which they can place orders with their chosen restaurant. The orders placed are not delivered by the restaurant's own delivery personnel; instead, the delivery company sends a delivery person to handle the delivery. Each delivery person operates under the company's colors, but the system manages different delivery personnel in different ways. It takes into account the mode of transportation used by the delivery person, with bicycles being the most common, but the company also employs delivery personnel using cars, motorcycles, and walking. Some companies even consider the quantity and quality of previously completed tasks when assigning personalized tasks to delivery personnel.

I spoke to an acquaintance who works as a bicycle delivery person for one such company and gained insight into their system and the interface that a delivery person working there sees. I learned about the approximate distances a delivery person typically travels for each address, the time the system allows for task completion, and that it's rare for delivery personnel to handle multiple orders simultaneously. It was valuable information to me that, although the system takes the delivery person's location into account, it doesn't prescribe the exact route. A delivery person only receives the two addresses: one for picking up the order and another for delivery.

This project was based on these insights. Rather than starting with an online algorithm, I worked on a task allocation problem that optimally assigns tasks to delivery personnel at a given moment. The goal was for the model to consider multiple factors beyond what delivery companies typically consider, and I aimed to visually represent an optimal solution to such a problem.

During the task, we had to solve a minimum weight perfect matching problem, implement multiple Dijkstra algorithms, and find an optimal solution to a more complex linear programming (LP) problem. And finally, we visualized the obtained results.

Representation:

Here is a courier's route depicted on the map.

The blue dot represents the courier's location, the red dot represents the customer's address, and finally, the green dot signifies the restaurant where the customer's order was placed. The orange line is the courier's shortest route, as you would expect.
